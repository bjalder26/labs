Safety:

  • Wear lab goggles at all times.
  • Wear nitrile gloves at all times.

Background:

  • Acid-base reactions are exothermic reactions that produce water molecules, and a salt. Salts are ionic compounds that dissolve in water, so there won’t be any visual confirmation of an acid-base reaction. However, a clue that an acid base reaction occurred is that heat is produced without a precipitate forming.
  • Certain transitional metal ions have colors that can be recognized fairly easily (see table 1 above).
  • Only certain combinations of ions will form precipitates (see table 2).
  • Temperature change: A noticeable change in temperature, either an increase (exothermic reaction) or decrease (endothermic reaction), can indicate the occurrence of a chemical reaction.
  • New scent: The production of a new odor or the change in scent during a reaction can be an indication of a chemical change. Some reactions can produce characteristic or distinctive smells.
  • Formation of light or a flame: Some reactions involve the production of light or the presence of a flame, indicating the release of energy or the occurrence of a combustion reaction.

Prelab Questions

  1. According to the solubility chart, is AgCl soluble in water?
  2. Which rule (or rule exception) helps you know if AgCl is soluble in water?
  3. According to the solubility chart, is Na2CO3 soluble in water?
  4. Which rule (or rule exception) helps you know if Na2CO3 is soluble in water?
  5. When aqueous solutions of BaCl2 and Na2SO4 are mixed, what precipitate is formed (put "none" if none are formed).
  6. When aqueous solutions of KNO3 and NaCl are mixed, what precipitate is formed (put "none" if none are formed).
  7. A small town has begun to worry about its drinking water after old lead pipes were discovered in part of the distribution system. These pipes may be leaching Pb²⁺ ions into the water supply, which can be harmful to residents. A chemist is asked to test a sample of the water by adding a solution that would form a visible precipitate if lead(II) ions are present. What aqueous compound could the chemist add to the water sample to test for the presence of Pb²⁺ ions?
  8. What precipitate formed in the previous question that would indicate contamination?
