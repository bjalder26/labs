Chemicals and lab equipment:

  • Spectrophotometer

  • 1x10-3 M potassium permanganate solution (~50 mL per group)
    Desired L x $\frac{1x10^{-3} M}{1 L}$ x $\frac{158.03 g}{1 mol}$ = needed g
  • unknown concentration potassium permanganate solution (1x10-4 M to 4x10-4 M) (~5 mL per group)

Each lab group:

  • (6) cuvettes or small test tubes (13 x 100mm)
  • (4) Large test tubes (16 x 100mm)
  • Test tube rack(s)
  • Wash bottle of deionized water
  • (2) - 10 mL graduated or pipets
  • Pipet pump
  • 100 mL beaker
  • 600 mL waste beaker
  • (4) squares of Parafilm or test tube stoppers
  • (6) plastic (Pasteur) pipets
  • Permanent marker
  • Labeling tape

Equations and Calculations:

  • Y = mX + b
    • m = slope, b = y-intercept
  • A = εbC
    • A = absorbance, ε = molar absorptivity (molar extinction coefficient), b = path length, C = concentration (molarity)
  • c = λν
    • c = 3.00 x 108m/s, λ = wavelength, ν = frequency

Safety:

  • Potassium permanganate is a strong oxidizing agent and can cause skin and eye irritation. Avoid direct contact with the skin or eyes. If contact occurs, rinse the affected area immediately with water.
  • Wear safety goggles or glasses at all times.
  • Wear nitrile gloves at all times.

Disposal:

  • Dilute to a very light pink color before disposing down the drain.

Procedure:

Part A: Creating standard solutions through dilution.

You will prepare a series of standard solutions. You will be provided with a 1x10-3 M standard solution of potassium permanganate. You will prepare standard solutions with a range of concentrations (4x10-4 M, 3x10-4 M, 2x10-4 M, and 1x10-4 M).
  1. Make sure the spectrophotometer is on – it can take 15 minutes to warm up. Make sure the mode is set to display absorbance rather than transmittance (if that is an option).
  2. Obtain approximately 50 mL of the 1x10-3 M potassium permanganate solution in your 100 mL beaker and label it “1x10-3 M”.
  3. Label 4 large test tubes “4x10-4 M”, “3x10-4 M”, “2x10-4 M” and “1x10-4 M”.
  4. Using the 10 mL graduated pipets add volumes of the 1x10-3 M potassium permanganate solution and deionized water (from your wash bottle) you obtained into each of the 4 labeled test tubes following the table below. Use one graduated pipet for the the 1x10-3 M potassium permanganate exclusively, and the other for the deionized water exclusively to prevent contamination.
