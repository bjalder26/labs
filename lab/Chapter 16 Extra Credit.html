    N2O4 (g) ⇌ 2 NO2 (g)

    Use these ideas to guide your reasoning:
    • Endothermic reactions absorb heat (treat heat like a reactant)
    • Exothermic reactions produce heat (treat heat as a product)
    • Increasing temperature shifts equilibrium away from the side that has heat
      • Toward the product in endothermic reactions
      • Toward the reactant in exothermic reactions
    • Decreasing temperature shifts equilibrium toward the side that has heat
      • Toward the reactant in endothermic reactions
      • Toward the product in exothermic reactions
    • Increasing pressure favors the side with fewer moles of gas
    • Decreasing pressure favors the side with more moles of gas
    • Increasing volume lowers the pressure
    • Decreasing volume increases the pressure
    • Catalysts do not have an effect on equilibrium, they only make you reach equilibrium faster
