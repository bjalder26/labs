  • Determine the correct cell notation for the redox reaction below.

    Zn (s) + Cu2+ (aq) ➞ Zn2+ (aq) + Cu (s)

    Use these ideas to guide your reasoning:
    • Oxidation occurs at the anode (loss of electrons)
    • Reduction occurs at the cathode (gain of electrons)
    • Anode is written on the left, cathode on the right
    • Format:
      • anode on the left, cathode on the right
      • | indicates a change in state (e.g. solid | aqueous or aqueous | solid)
      • || represents the salt bridge
      • Example: anode | ion || ion | solid

    Step 1: Identify oxidation

    Which species is oxidized?


    Step 2: Identify reduction

    Which species is reduced?


    Step 3: Choose the correct cell notation



    Explanation:
    • Zn is oxidized to Zn²⁺, so it is the anode (left side).
    • Cu²⁺ is reduced to Cu, so it is the cathode (right side).
    • Zn(s) and Zn²⁺(aq) are not in the same state, so there is a | between them.
    • Cu²⁺(aq) and Cu(s) are not in the same state, so there is a | between them.
    • There is a salt bridge between the ions so there must be a || between them.
