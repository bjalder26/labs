Background

A system at equilibrium has equal forward and reverse reaction rates, so there is no net change in concentrations. However, the reaction is still active and can respond to changes.

Le Châtelier’s Principle states that when a stress is applied to a system at equilibrium, the system shifts in the direction that reduces that stress.

1. Changes in Concentration

Adding a reactant or removing a product shifts the reaction to the right (toward products). Adding a product or removing a reactant shifts the reaction to the left (toward reactants).

2. Changes in Temperature

Temperature affects equilibrium depending on whether the reaction is endothermic or exothermic:

  • Increasing temperature favors the endothermic direction.
  • Decreasing temperature favors the exothermic direction.

Observing Equilibrium Shifts

  • Color changes: Some equilibria involve colored species, so shifts can be seen by color changes.
  • Precipitation: Formation or dissolution of a solid indicates a shift in equilibrium.

Example equilibrium:

Ca(OH)2(s) ⇌ Ca 2+(aq) + 2OH -(aq)

In this lab, you will apply changes in concentration and temperature to several systems and observe how the equilibrium responds.

Procedure

For Parts I and II, prepare 12 clean, dry test tubes labeled 1–12 in a test tube rack. Label the small test tubes 1-11, and the largest test tube 12.

Prepare three water baths (this should already be ready, but make sure so that you don't have to wait for them):

  • 2 Hot water baths: Fill a 250 mL beaker about 2/3 full with water and heat on a hot plate (do not boil dry).
  • Ice bath: Fill a 250 mL beaker with ice and add water to create a slush.

I. Colorimetric Changes to Monitor Equilibrium Shifts

Iron(III) and Thiocyanate

Fe(H2O)6 3+(aq) + SCN -(aq) ⇌ Fe(H2O)5(SCN) 2+(aq) + H2O(l)

Fe(H2O)6 3+ appears gold           Fe(H2O)5(SCN) 2+ appears dark red/brown

A. Set Up the Equilibrium
  1. Fill test tube 1 about 2/3 full with deionized water.
  2. Add 1 drop of 0.2 M Fe(NO3)3 and 2 drops of 0.1 M KSCN. Mix well. You can use parafilm, then shake it, or tap it until it mixes.
  3. Solution should appear red-orange.
  4. Divide this solution evenly into tubes 1–5.
    5. B. Apply Changes
  5. Tube 1: control (no change). Record appearance below.
  6. Tube 2: add 2 drops Fe(NO3)3; mix and observe. Record appearance below.
  7. Tube 3: add 1-2 drops KSCN; mix and observe. Record appearance below.
  8. Tube 4: add 1–2 drops AgNO3 ( use a test tube holder); mix and observe. Record appearance below. Note, if you get a white precipitate then ignore it and focus on the color change of the aqueous portion. A white precipitate indicates contamination.
  9. Tube 5: heat in hot water bath (3–5 min); observe. Record appearance below.
  10. Make sure to compare test tubes 2-5 to the unchanged tube 1.
    11. Cobalt(II) and Chloride
