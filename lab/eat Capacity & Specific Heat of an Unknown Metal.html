Procedure:

Part A: Measuring the heat capacity of your coffee cup calorimeter.

You will mix hot water with room temperature water, measure the temperature of the water before and after mixing, then calculate the heat capacity of the coffee cup calorimeter.
  1. Fill the 600 mL beaker with approximately 500 mL of tap water, and place it on a hot plate set on a high setting.
    • Assign one person in your lab group to keep an eye on the boiling water.
    • Monitor the temperature of the hot water bath, and try to keep it between 80°C and 85°C
    • Add more water to the hot water bath as needed
    • Stir the hot water bath occasionally with a stirring rod (not with the thermometer)
  2. While your hot water bath is heating, assemble the coffee cups, lid, and magnetic stirring rod.
  3. Using the 100 mL graduated cylinder, measure out 70 mL of deionized water.
  4. Place the coffee cups, lid, and magnetic stirring rod on the scale and tare the scale.
  5. Add the 70 mL of deionized water to the coffee cups on the scale, and measure the mass.
  6. Record the mass of your "cold" (room temperature) water in table 1.
  7. Assemble your coffee cup calorimeter on top of your magnetic stirring plate with the temperature probe held by a 3 prong clamp slightly above your stir bar.
  8. Experiment with your magnetic stirring plate to make sure it will not spin so fast that the temperature probe's tip will not be in the water. Turn the stirring plate of once you've determined the correct speed to set the spin.
  9. Using the 100 mL graduated cylinder, measure out 30 mL of deionized water.
  10. Place a 125 mL erlenmeyer flask on the scale and tare the scale.
  11. Pour the 30 mL of water into the 125 mL Erlenmeyer flask on the scale.
  12. Record the mass of the 30 mL of water in table 1.
  13. Suspend the 125 mL Erlenmeyer flask in the hot water by placing the neck in a utility clamp.
  14. Submurse the 125 mL Erlenmeyer flast to the point where the water level in the flask is below the water level in your hot beaker and bring the temperature of the water in the flast to between 80°C and 85°C (no hotter).
