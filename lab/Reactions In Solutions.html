Procedure: Part A: Double displacement reactions You will mix aqueous solutions in 4 different wells, and record your observations looking for color changes, precipitation (solids on the bottom or cloudiness), gas formation, and temperature change. Label your well plate as shown above. 2) For each reaction measure the temperature before and after adding the 2nd reactant. If the temperature change is more than 0.2°C, then record the temperature change in the table below. 3) Add 10-12 drops of sodium hydroxide and iron(III) chloride to well 1 and record your observations in the table below. 4) Add 10-12 drops of sodium chloride and silver nitrate to well 2 and record your observations in the table below. 5) Add 10-12 drops of sodium carbonate and nickel(II) nitrate to well 3 and record your observations in the table below. 6) Add 10-12 drops of sodium acetate and lead(II) nitrate to well 4 and record your observations in the table below. 7) Based on your observations, decide if there was no reaction, if a precipitate or gas was formed, or if the reaction was exothermic or endothermic. If the temperature change was greater than 0.2°C and positive, then indicate that the reaction was exothermic, if the temperature change was greater than 0.2°C and negative, then indicate that the reaction was endothermic.
