PART 2: Compare

Directions:

Switch the simulation to the Compare screen. Make sure Same Mass is clicked in the top right corner.

Question 15: Put one block in the water at a time. What do you notice? Remove the block from the water. Repeat this process with every other block. Indicate using the letter of the block whether they sink or float.

Blocks C and D immediately sink. Block B floats. Block A sort of floats in the middle.

Question 16:Adjust the mass to 8.50 kg. Which block now sinks that didn't previously?

Block A now sinks.

Question 17: Adjust the mass to 1.80 kg. Which blocks now float?

Blocks A, B, and C now float.

Question 18: Reset the mass to 5.00 kg. Experiment with putting different blocks on top of each other. Without adjusting the mass are there ways you can make a block sink that would normally float by doing this?

You can make Block B sink by placing two blocks on top of it.

Question 19: What about making a block float that would normally sink?

You can make Blocks C or D float by placing it on top of Block B.

Question 20: Block B, the biggest block and Block D, the smallest block have the same mass. But one floats and one sinks. Why do you think that is?

Block B, the biggest block has a higher volume. Thus even though it has the same amount of mass it can still float.

Now click on the same volume in the top right corner of the simulation.

Question 21: Throw all four blocks into the water. Which ones float? Which ones don’t? Why do you think that is? Use the masses of the blocks to indicate which ones sink and which ones float.

The 2.0 and 4.0 kg ones float, but the 6.0 kg and 8.0 kg ones sink. The heavier ones have too much density to float.

Questions 22: Decrease the volume of the blocks to 1.50 L. What happens to the blocks?

They all sink.

Question 23: Now increase the volume to 10.0 L. What happens to the blocks?

They all float.

Question 24: Reset the volume to 5.00 L. Experiment with putting different blocks on top of each other. Are there ways you can make a block sink that would normally float by doing this? What about making a block float that would normally sink?

Answers will vary, but should focus on putting the heavier ones on top of the lighter ones. This will either make the heavier one float, or the lighter one sink.

Question 25: All four blocks have the same volume, but they have different masses. But some float and some sink. Why do you think that is?

If they have the same volume but different masses, they have different densities. If they have a density lower than water, they can float. If they have a density higher than water, they will sink.
