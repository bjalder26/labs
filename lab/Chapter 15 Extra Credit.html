    Guided Problem

    Which law of thermodynamics states that the total entropy of the universe increases for any spontaneous process?

  1. Reference: Laws of Thermodynamics
    • Zeroth Law: If two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third system, they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.
    • First Law: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred or converted from one form to another.
    • Second Law: The total entropy of the universe increases for any spontaneous process.
    • Third Law: The entropy of a perfect crystalline substance is zero at absolute zero temperature.

    3. Guided Problem

  3. Consider the following reaction at constant pressure. Use the information below to determine the value of ΔSsurr at K. Then predict whether or not this reaction will be spontaneous at this temperature.

    2 KClO3(s) ➞ 2 KCl(s) + 3 O2(g), ΔH = −89.4 kJ

    Step-by-step reasoning

    Step 1: Convert enthalpy change into J.

    ΔH = J

    Step 2: Calculate the entropy change of the surroundings

    $ \Delta S_{\text{surr}} = -\frac{\Delta H_{sys}}{T} $

    ΔSsurr = J/K

    Step 3: Consider the entropy change of the system

    This reaction produces gas from solids, so the entropy of the system increases (ΔSsys > 0).

    ΔSuni = ΔSsys + ΔSsurr
    If the ΔSuni is positive, then the reaction is spontaneous. If the ΔSuni is negative, then the reaction is nonspontaneous.
    Since both ΔSsys and ΔSsurr are positive, the reaction is
    at this temperature.
