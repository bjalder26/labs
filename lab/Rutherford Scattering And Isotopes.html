Rutherford Scattering and Isotopes Lab

PART 1: Rutherford Atom

Question 1: What do you notice about the paths of most of the alpha particles when the number of protons is set to 20?

Question 2: Increase the number of protons to 60. Does it change how the alpha particles move? If so, how?

Question 3: Now increase the number of protons to 100. How does this increase change how the alpha particles move through the gold foil as compared to when you started? Why do you think this change occurred?

Question 4: Repeat the above steps but vary how many neutrons are present with the protons. Does this change how the Alpha particles travel? Why or why not?

Question 5: Set the number of protons to 20. Watch and describe how the alpha particles move in relationship to the nucleus.

Question 6: Set the number of protons to 60. Watch and describe how the alpha particles move in relationship to the nucleus.

Question 7: Set the number of protons to 100. Watch and describe how the alpha particles move in relationship to the nucleus.

Question 8: How are the situations in #5 (only 20 protons) and #7 (100 protons) different? Why do you think this is?

Question 9: What type of path do the Alpha particles take in the Plum-Pudding model?

Question 10: How is this different from the Rutherford model?

PART 3: ISOTOPES

Question 13: Define “isotope” using the following words in your definition: mass number, atomic number, number of protons, neutrons, and electrons.

Question 14: Compare and contrast an element, an atom, and an isotope.
