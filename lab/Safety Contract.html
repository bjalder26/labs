General Chemistry Laboratory Safety Contract

Name:

Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this safety contract is to prevent chemical exposure, physical injury, fires, and environmental harm during General Chemistry laboratory activities. Chemistry laboratories contain hazards that are not immediately obvious, including toxic chemicals, corrosive substances, open flames, pressurized systems, and fragile glassware. Many laboratory accidents occur not due to lack of intelligence, but due to carelessness, distractions, or failure to follow established safety procedures.

By agreeing to this contract, I affirm that I understand these risks and agree to follow all safety rules, instructions, and professional standards at all times while in the laboratory.

1. Personal Responsibility and Conduct

I will follow all written and verbal instructions exactly and immediately.
Reason: Laboratory procedures are designed to control risk. Deviating from instructions—even in small ways—can create unexpected chemical reactions, exposure hazards, or equipment failures.
I will not perform unauthorized experiments or modify procedures.
Reason: Chemicals and equipment behave predictably only under known conditions. Unauthorized changes remove safety controls and place everyone in the lab at risk.
Horseplay, practical jokes, running, or distracting behavior are strictly prohibited.
Reason: Many laboratory hazards cause injury through brief, accidental contact. Distractions increase the likelihood of spills, collisions, or improper handling.
I will remain alert and focused for the entire laboratory period.
Reason: Fatigue, inattention, or multitasking leads to delayed responses during spills, fires, or chemical exposure incidents.

2. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Approved safety goggles must be worn at all times when chemicals, heat, or glassware are in use.
Reason: Eye tissue is extremely vulnerable to chemical burns and flying fragments. Many eye injuries occur when students “just take goggles off for a moment.”
Closed-toe shoes, long pants, and secured long hair are required.
Reason: Spilled chemicals and broken glass fall downward. Exposed skin and loose hair significantly increase injury risk.
Gloves will be worn when required and removed before touching common surfaces.
Reason: Gloves protect skin but can spread contamination if worn outside the lab work area.

3. Chemical Handling and Use

I will never taste chemicals or intentionally smell chemicals directly.
Reason: Many chemicals are toxic even in small amounts, and some cause harm without immediate sensory warning. When instructed to smell, proper wafting techniques must be used.
All chemicals will be treated as hazardous.
Reason: Even substances labeled “dilute” or “common” can cause injury through improper use or mixing.
I will read chemical labels carefully and use only the amounts specified.
Reason: Excess chemicals increase risk, complicate disposal, and can lead to violent reactions.
No chemicals may be returned to their original containers.
Reason: Returning chemicals can contaminate stock supplies, creating hidden hazards for future users.

4. Glassware, Equipment, and Heat Sources

I will inspect glassware for cracks or defects before use.
Reason: Damaged glass is more likely to fail under stress, causing cuts or chemical splashes.
Hot glass will be treated as if it is hot at all times.
Reason: Hot glass looks identical to cool glass and can cause severe burns.
Open flames and heating devices will never be left unattended.
Reason: Unattended heat sources are a leading cause of laboratory fires.

5. Laboratory Environment and Hygiene

Food, drink, gum, cosmetics, and lip balm are strictly prohibited in the lab.
Reason: Chemicals can be transferred from hands or air onto items that go into the mouth or contact skin.
Hands must be washed thoroughly before leaving the laboratory.
Reason: Chemical residues may not be visible but can still cause harm through later skin contact or ingestion.
Work areas must be kept clean and organized.
Reason: Clutter increases the likelihood of spills, breakage, and accidental reactions.
Bags and personal items (no food or drinks) must be stored in the cubbies and off the floor.
Reason: Personal items on the floor are a tripping hazard.

6. Accidents, Spills, and Emergencies

All accidents, spills, injuries, or unsafe conditions must be reported immediately.
Reason: Minor incidents can escalate if not addressed promptly, and early treatment reduces injury severity.
I will follow emergency procedures exactly as instructed.
Reason: Laboratory emergency equipment and procedures are designed for coordinated response, not individual improvisation.

7. Compliance and Consequences

Failure to follow this safety contract may result in removal from the laboratory.
Reason: Unsafe behavior endangers not only the individual, but everyone in the lab.
Repeated or severe violations may result in dismissal from the course.
Reason: Laboratory safety depends on consistent compliance and professional responsibility.
