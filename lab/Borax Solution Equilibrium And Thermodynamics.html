Borax Solution Equilibrium and Thermodynamics

Name:

Objectives

  • Determine the solibility product constant (Ksp) for the dissolution of borax at different temperatures.
  • Determine the values of ΔG° for the dissolution of borax at different temperatures.
  • Estimate the values of ΔH° and ΔS° for the dissolution of borax at 25°C.

Materials

  • Deionized water
  • Ice
  • Borax (Sodium tetraborate decahydrate - Na2B4O7 · 10H2O or Na2[B4O5(OH4)] · 8H2O)
  • Methyl red indicator
  • 0.5 M HCl solution
  • 250 mL Erlenmeyer flask (1)
  • 100 or 125 mL Erlenmeyer flasks (4)
  • 100 mL beaker
  • 100 mL graduated cylinder
  • 600 mL beaker
  • 50 mL buret and buret stand
  • Magnetic stirring bar
  • Hot plate
  • 5 mL volumetric pipet & pipet pump
  • Thermometer or temperature probe
  • Ring stand with clamp for thermometer
  • Heat resistant gloves
  • Beaker tongs
  • A beaker of heated water to clean glassware (~50°C)

Safety Precautions

  • Safety goggles should be worn at all times.
  • Hot glassware can cause burns. Use heat resistant gloves or beaker tongs
  • HCl is caustic and can damage skin - wear nitril gloves.

Procedure

  1. Heated water will be provided to help students wash glassware, and unclog pipets. Washing your glassware as soon as possible may prevent the need of heated water to clean it.
  2. Measure about 25g of borax and place it into a 250 mL Erlenmeyer flask.
  3. Add a stirring bar and 75 mL of deionized water to the flask.
Steps for groups below room temperature
  1. Place your Erlenmeyer flask in and ice-water bath and stir for at least 20 minutes. Some undissolved solid should remain to ensure that the solution is saturated.
  2. If your entire sample dissolves, then add a little more solid until a small amount remains undissolved
  3. Stop stirring, place a thermometer or temperature probe in the flask, and wait 10 more minutes for the undissolved solid to settle.
  4. Keep the flask in the ice-water bath until it is time to remove the 5 mL samples.
Steps for groups at room temperature
  1. Gently stir your solution for 10 minutes.
  2. Some undissolved solid should remain to ensure that the solution is saturated.
  3. If your entire sample dissolves, then add a little more solid until a small amount remains undissolved
  4. Stop stirring, place a thermometer or temperature probe in the flask, and wait 10 more minutes for the undissolved solid to settle.
Steps for groups below room temperature
  1. Place your Erlenmeyer flask on a hot plate, place a thermometer or temperature probe in the flask, and heat the mixture while stirring until you reach about 5 degrees above your target temperature.
  2. Maintain that temperature by turning the hot plate off and on, or by removing and replacing the flask for 10 minutes while stiring. Some undissolved solid should remain to ensure that the solution is saturated.
  3. If your entire sample dissolves, then add a little more solid until a small amount remains undissolved
  4. Turn off the hot plate, and remove your flask from the heat.
  1. Once you begin removing samples (near your target temperature) record the exact temperature.
  2. Using the 5 mL volumetric pipet, quickly remove four 5.00 mL samples from your 250 mL flask, and place each of them in a seperate 100 (or 125) mL Erlenmeyer flask.
  3. Add 15 mL of deionized water and 2-3 drops of methyl red indicator to each of the 100 (or 125) mL flasks.
  4. Wash your 250 mL flask, stirring rod(s), and pipet(s). DO NOT wash your 100 (or 125) mL flask samples. Borax can be difficult to wash away if left in contact with glassware for too long, and you may need to use the heated water.
  5. Fill a buret with 0.5 M HCl solution.
  6. Open the stopcock and allow a few milliliters of HCl to run through the tip into a 600 mL waste beaker.
  7. Titrate each of your 4 samples with the HCl following these steps:
    1. Place a clean magnetic stirring rod in the 100 (or 125) mL flask and put it on the magnetic stirrer.
    2. Start the magnetic stirrer at a low rate.
    3. Record your initial buret reading - this may be zero if you refilled your buret or are using a drop counter.
    4. Begin adding HCl drop-by-drop while watching for pink-ish puffs of color.
    5. The larger the pinkinsh area before disappearing, the closer you are to the end point, and you should slow the titration down.
    6. When you get a persistant pinkish color, you have reached the end point. Try to get the lightest pink possible. You may need to put a piece of white paper behind your sample to check the color.
    7. Your titration is NOT COMPLETE if there is still dissolved solids at the bottom of your flask.
    8. Record your final volume.
    9. Calculate and record the volume of HCl added to your borax solution.
    10. If this is not the last sample, then refil your buret, and repeat these steps with the next sample.
  8. Dispose of your samples down the drain, and pour the remaining HCl solution in a waste container provided by your instructor.
